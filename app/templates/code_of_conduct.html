Code of Conduct

The Lost and Found office at the college is committed to integrity, transparency, and the respectful handling of all lost property. This Code of Conduct outlines expectations for all individuals managing or assisting with lost and found operations.

Core Principles

  • Integrity – Treat all found items with honesty and care.
  • Accountability – Maintain accurate records and follow all procedures.
  • Respect – Treat all individuals seeking lost items with professionalism and courtesy.
  • Confidentiality – Protect the identity and privacy of individuals who report, retrieve, or are associated with found items.

Responsibilities of Staff and Volunteers

Collection & Intake of Items

  • All found items must be turned in as soon as possible to the designated Lost and Found location.
  • Staff must log each item into the tracking system with full details.
  • Items should be sorted and stored based on type (general, valuables, perishables).

Valuables

  • Stored securely in a locked location with limited access.
  • Two staff members must be present during intake.
  • Record serial numbers, identifying marks, and take internal photos.
  • Attempt contact via official channels if ID is available.
  • Phones must not be unlocked by staff; owners must verify identity via biometric if necessary.
  • After 90 days, unclaimed valuables follow college policy or go to Campus Security.

Claiming Items

  • Owner must provide valid ID or item description.
  • Signed release form required before returning items.
  • Items only returned to rightful owners or authorized individuals with proof.

Confidentiality and Privacy

  • Do not share lost item info with unauthorized individuals.
  • Handle personal data (e.g., IDs, cards) in line with college/state privacy policies.

Prohibited Conduct

  • Keeping, using, or tampering with found items.
  • Failure to report valuable or sensitive items.
  • Sharing lost item photos or details publicly/social media.
  • Using Lost and Found access for personal gain or favoritism.

Compliance and Reporting

  • All staff and volunteers must complete annual training.
  • Report misconduct to Campus Security or Student Affairs immediately.
  • Violations may result in disciplinary action and removal from the program.

Acknowledgment

All participants in the Lost and Found process must sign an acknowledgment form agreeing to comply with this Code of Conduct and its procedures.
